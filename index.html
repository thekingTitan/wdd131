Lagos, Nigeria

Lagos, Nigeria’s largest city, is a vibrant metropolis known for its rich history, cultural diversity, and economic significance. Founded in 1452 and named by Portuguese explorers, Lagos has evolved into Africa's economic powerhouse and innovation hub, contributing 25% of Nigeria’s GDP. It’s the birthplace of Afrobeats and home to iconic landmarks like the Third Mainland Bridge and the NECOM House. Lagos boasts a dynamic food scene with staples like Jollof rice and Suya, while its festivals celebrate creativity and tradition. Despite its challenges, the energy and resilience of Lagosians make the city a true symbol of African excellence.
